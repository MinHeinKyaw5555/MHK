    Our Coffee Blends

    Black Velvet

    Black Velvet is a smooth, full-bodied dark roast with notes of caramel, toasted cocoa, and a silky finish—never bitter. Perfect for French Press, espresso, or a bold drip.

    Tasting Notes

    Burnt sugar, graham cracker, malted milk balls

    Brewing Methods

    Best brewed with French Press or Espresso. Use fresh ground beans and water around 93°C (200°F) for optimal flavor.

    $5.00
    House Blend

    What can we say about House? Well, let’s see. It’s a medium roast. It tastes like coffee. Really good coffee. The kind you actually want to drink every day. You don’t need a flavor wheel—just a mug. This is House.

    Tasting Notes

    nutty, caramel, cocoa bean

    Brewing Methods

    Drip gives a smooth, easy everyday cup. Pour-over brings out its clean and balanced flavor. French Press adds more body and richness.

    Tasting Notes

    clementine, nougat, burnt sugar

    Colombia Huila

    Sweet, juicy, and endlessly drinkable, this coffee from Huila exemplifies why Colombia is synonymous with coffee. Expect bright acidity, red apple sweetness, and a rich molasses finish.

    Tasting Notes

    Brewing Methods

    Burundi Bahire

    Bright, juicy, and comforting—Bahire is a dynamic cup that evolves as it cools. Grown on a single hillside in Burundi’s Ngozi province, this sweet and complex lot brings together delicate florals, stone fruit, raisin, and smooth chocolatey depth. Named after the Kirundi word for “be well,” Bahire is a coffee that feels as uplifting as it is layered.

    Tasting Notes

    peach snapple, raisinets, pastry crust

    Brewing Methods

    Pour-over brings out its bright citrus and floral notes. Chemex offers clarity and a delicate, tea-like body. Aeropress balances fruitiness with a smooth finish.

    $5.50
